Sable Island Seals

Sable Island in Newfoundland Canada is a National Park Reserve off the coast of Nova Scotia. This twelve mile long crescent, isolated in the Atlantic, is known for its incredible wildlife from wild horses to birds and uniquely adapted insects. The world's largest grey seal breeding colony, some 400,000 strong, flourish on this remote island home. In June of 2011, scientists tagged a handful of Sable Island seals with GPS devices and tracked their movement for six months in intimate detail.
