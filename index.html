Sable Island Seals

Sable Island in Newfoundland Canada is a National Park Reserve off the coast of Nova Scotia. It is approximatly 12 miles long and is known for its incredible wild life. It is home to 500 wildhorses and 400,000 Grey Seals use it as a hub. Being so remote has allowed these animals to flourish. This page allows you to see how a hand full of seals interact with the island and surrounding area. To learn more about Sable Island visit their national park webpage at: https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ns/sable
